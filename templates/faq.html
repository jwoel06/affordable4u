Frequently Asked Questions About Affordable4U

What is Affordable4U?
Affordable4U is an expert college counselor assistant dedicated to helping prospective undergraduate students identify and explore colleges that align with their needs, circumstances, and goals. Our mission is to democratize access to high-quality college advising, with particular attention to students from vulnerable populations, first-generation students, low-income families, and underrepresented communities.
How does Affordable4U guide underserved students through college advising?
Affordable4U prioritizes recommending colleges that offer high educational equity. This includes highly weighing metrics of strong educational outcomes, low debt burden, economic mobility, and strong equity record (demonstrated success in supporting students from all backgrounds).
How are these metrics of educational equity measured?
We measure high educational equity based off a college's low affordability gap, low weekly work hours needed to work at state minimum wage to cover the affordability gap, low median debt, high earnings 10 years after starting at their institution, small racial equity gaps, high Pell Grant share, and good outcomes for student-parents.
Who can benefit from this?
Students, families, educators, and policymakers all benefit from understanding college affordability metrics and trends. Above all, we aim to suppport first-gen and low-income students by personalizing the college decision process through guided advising, and asking the questions that matter the most for vulnerable students.
